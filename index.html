Zksync is a layer 2 scaling solution for the Ethereum blockchain designed to address its scalability limitations. It utilizes a technology called zkRollups, which leverages zero-knowledge proofs to enable fast and low-cost transactions while maintaining the security of the Ethereum network.

With zkSync, multiple transactions are bundled together and a cryptographic proof is generated to represent them. This proof is then published on the Ethereum mainnet, reducing the amount of data that needs to be stored on-chain. This approach significantly increases transaction throughput and reduces fees compared to executing transactions directly on the Ethereum mainnet.

Transferring funds from the Ethereum mainnet to zkSync involves a deposit process, where users lock their assets on the mainnet and mint corresponding zkSync tokens. These tokens can be freely transferred and traded within the zkSync network. Users can interact with decentralized applications (DApps) that integrate with zkSync, such as decentralized exchanges, yield farming platforms, and more.

zkSync Era and zkSync Lite?

At its inception, zkSync lacked support for smart contracts, preventing activities such as using DeFi or purchasing NFTs, which are common on Ethereum. However, it did provide significant scaling for Ethereum, leading to its nickname “zkSync Lite.

The zkSync Era represents an upgrade to the network, delivering all the features of Ethereum while also providing more cost-effective and faster transactions.
